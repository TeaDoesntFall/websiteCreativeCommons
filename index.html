Of the world's 7.2 billion people, about 1.2 billion live in extreme poverty and nearly half of the world's population is considered moderately poor.

In the year ended June 2021, 13.6 percent of New Zealand children (156,700) lived in households with less than 50 percent of the median equivalised disposable household income before deducting housing costs.
